Why Privacy Matters

Privacy isn't about hiding wrongdoing. It's about control ‚Äî your control over information about yourself. When you lose privacy, you lose the ability to make decisions without being judged, manipulated, or discriminated against.

Your data is used to build profiles that predict your behavior, influence your political views, determine insurance rates, affect job applications, and target you with psychological advertising. You are the product, not the customer.

"Saying you don't care about privacy because you have nothing to hide is like saying you don't care about free speech because you have nothing to say." ‚Äî Edward Snowden

Privacy protects everyone ‚Äî not just people doing something wrong. It protects journalists, activists, abuse survivors, LGBTQ+ individuals in hostile environments, and anyone who simply doesn't want corporations profiling their every move.

What Data Is Collected About You

By websites and apps:

  • ‚Ä¢Every page you visit, how long you stay, what you click
  • ‚Ä¢Your IP address (reveals your approximate location and ISP)
  • ‚Ä¢Browser fingerprint (screen size, fonts, plugins ‚Äî uniquely identifies you)
  • ‚Ä¢Search queries and browsing history
  • ‚Ä¢Location data (GPS, WiFi triangulation)
  • ‚Ä¢Purchase history and browsing habits

By social media:

  • ‚Ä¢All posts, likes, messages (even deleted ones, stored on servers)
  • ‚Ä¢Your social graph ‚Äî who you know and how close you are
  • ‚Ä¢Face recognition data (if you upload photos)
  • ‚Ä¢Tracking pixels on third-party sites (Facebook tracks you even when you're not on Facebook)
Even if you've never had a Facebook account, Facebook builds "shadow profiles" on you using data from websites with Facebook Pixel and from contacts uploaded by other users.

Passwords & Accounts

Weak and reused passwords are the #1 cause of account compromises. Here's what to do:

üîë
Use a Password Manager Essential
Bitwarden (free, open source) or KeePassXC (local-only)
Generates and stores unique 20+ character passwords for every site. You only remember one master password.
üì±
Enable 2FA on Every Important Account Essential
Use an authenticator app (Aegis on Android, Raivo on iOS)
Even if your password is stolen, attackers can't get in without the second factor. Avoid SMS 2FA ‚Äî use TOTP apps instead.
Most important accounts to secure with 2FA: Email (controls everything else), banking, social media, cloud storage, and your password manager.

Network Privacy

VPNs Intermediate

A VPN encrypts your traffic and hides your activity from your ISP. But it shifts trust ‚Äî your VPN provider can see your traffic instead. Choose carefully: Mullvad and ProtonVPN have independently audited no-logs policies.

VPN myths: VPNs don't make you anonymous. Websites can still track you via cookies, fingerprinting, and account logins. A VPN primarily protects you from ISP monitoring and hides your IP from websites.

Public WiFi

Public WiFi is generally safe for HTTPS traffic ‚Äî your browser encrypts data in transit. The main risks are evil twin attacks (fake hotspots) and unencrypted HTTP sites. Always verify you're on the real network and use a VPN if you're accessing sensitive accounts.
